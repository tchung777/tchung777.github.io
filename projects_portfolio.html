Project Portfolio

Welcome to my project portfolio. In here are several projects that I have worked on or helped worked on. This section is currently a WIP. Please come back later when I have updated with projects that I have worked on in the past as well as currently!


Project Micromouse 2016 - Santa Clara University

The Micromouse project was for a competition hosted by IEEE where you build a robotic mouse to solve a 16x16 grid maze. To solve it, you have to get to the center of the maze and then back track to your starting spot. The robot needs to take less than 10 minutes to complete this. You could not fly, destroy, or use the top of the walls as markings. So obviously there was some design restraints and tech specifications we had to follow.
This was the first serious project I took on outside of lab work.

Tiny House 2016 - Santa Clara University

I was part of the Tiny House team at Santa Clara University when they participated in the 2016 Tiny House Competition hosted by SMUD. Our tiny house was 238 sq.ft and was powered by sunlight. It was completely "off the grid" and utilized salt water batteries to store excessive energy during the daytime for night use. I was of part the electrical/computer engineering team on the rEvolve House team.
The most difficult part of this project was building the actual house. As consumer product designers, the trend is generally that the larger the scale of the project, the more difficult. That is why we benefited from having leaders who were actually versed in house building. Ultimately, we were able to put together the house as a team. Visit the rEvolveHouse website for more information on the project!
